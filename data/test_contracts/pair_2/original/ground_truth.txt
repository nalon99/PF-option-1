RESIDENTIAL REAL ESTATE PURCHASE AGREEMENT

This Residential Real Estate Purchase Agreement (the "Agreement") is made and 
entered into as of March 15, 2024 (the "Effective Date"), by and between 
Michael R. Thompson and Sarah L. Thompson (collectively, the "Buyer"), and 
James K. Morrison (the "Seller").

---

I. PROPERTY DESCRIPTION

1.1. Property Address. The Seller agrees to sell, and the Buyer agrees to 
purchase, the real property located at 742 Maple Street, Riverside Heights, 
State of California, 92501 (the "Property").

1.2. Legal Description. The Property is legally described as Lot 24, Block 7, 
of Riverside Heights Subdivision, as recorded in Book 145, Page 312, of the 
Official Records of Riverside County, California.

1.3. Included Items. The sale includes all fixtures permanently attached to 
the Property, including but not limited to: built-in appliances, ceiling fans, 
window treatments, landscaping, and the existing security system.

---

II. PURCHASE PRICE AND PAYMENT TERMS

2.1. Purchase Price. The total purchase price for the Property shall be 
**Four Hundred Seventy-Five Thousand Dollars ($475,000 USD)**.

2.2. Earnest Money Deposit. Within three (3) business days of the Effective 
Date, Buyer shall deposit **Fifteen Thousand Dollars ($15,000 USD)** as 
earnest money with Pacific Coast Escrow Services (the "Escrow Agent").
2.3. Financing Terms. The Buyer intends to obtain a conventional mortgage 
loan in the amount of **Three Hundred Eighty Thousand Dollars ($380,000 USD)**, 
representing approximately 80% of the purchase price. The Buyer shall have 
**twenty-one (21) days** from the Effective Date to obtain a written loan 
commitment from a qualified lender.

2.4. Down Payment. The balance of the purchase price, less the earnest money 
deposit, shall be paid in cash or certified funds at closing.

2.5. Failure to Obtain Financing. If the Buyer, despite good faith efforts, 
is unable to obtain financing approval within the specified period, either 
party may terminate this Agreement, and the earnest money shall be returned 
to the Buyer in full.

---

III. INSPECTIONS AND DUE DILIGENCE

3.1. Inspection Period. The Buyer shall have **ten (10) days** from the 
Effective Date to conduct, at Buyer's expense, any inspections deemed 
necessary, including but not limited to: general home inspection, pest 
inspection, roof inspection, and environmental assessments.

3.2. Inspection Contingency. If the inspections reveal material defects 
that the Seller is unwilling to repair or credit, the Buyer may terminate 
this Agreement within the inspection period and receive a full refund of 
the earnest money deposit.

3.3. Seller's Disclosure. The Seller shall provide a completed Property 
Disclosure Statement within five (5) days of the Effective Date, disclosing 
all known material defects affecting the Property.
IV. TITLE AND CLOSING

4.1. Title Insurance. The Seller shall provide, at Seller's expense, a 
standard owner's title insurance policy issued by a reputable title company, 
insuring the Buyer's title to the Property in the amount of the purchase price.

4.2. Clear Title. The Seller warrants that at closing, the Property shall 
be conveyed with clear and marketable title, free from all liens, encumbrances, 
and defects, except for: current year property taxes not yet due, recorded 
easements and restrictions of record, and zoning ordinances.

4.3. Closing Date. The closing of this transaction shall occur on or before 
**April 30, 2024**, unless extended by mutual written agreement of the parties.

4.4. Closing Costs. The Seller shall pay: real estate commissions, documentary 
transfer taxes, and one-half of the escrow fees. The Buyer shall pay: loan 
origination fees, appraisal fees, recording fees, and one-half of the escrow fees.

---

V. PROPERTY CONDITION AND POSSESSION

5.1. Property Condition. The Seller agrees to maintain the Property in its 
present condition, ordinary wear and tear excepted, and shall deliver the 
Property in broom-clean condition at closing.

5.2. Risk of Loss. The Seller shall bear all risk of loss or damage to the 
Property until the closing date. If the Property is substantially damaged 
prior to closing, the Buyer may terminate this Agreement.
5.3. Possession. The Buyer shall be entitled to possession of the Property 
at **5:00 PM on the closing date**, following the recording of the deed and 
disbursement of funds.

5.4. Final Walkthrough. The Buyer shall have the right to conduct a final 
walkthrough inspection of the Property within **twenty-four (24) hours** 
prior to closing to verify the condition of the Property.

---

VI. REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES

6.1. Seller's Representations. The Seller represents and warrants that:
(a) The Seller has full authority to sell the Property;
(b) There are no pending or threatened legal actions affecting the Property;
(c) All mechanical systems and appliances are in working order;
(d) The Property complies with all applicable zoning and building codes.

6.2. Buyer's Representations. The Buyer represents and warrants that:
(a) The Buyer has the financial capacity to complete this transaction;
(b) The Buyer has reviewed and accepted the Property Disclosure Statement;
(c) The Buyer is not relying on any representations not contained herein.

---

VII. DEFAULT AND REMEDIES

7.1. Buyer's Default. If the Buyer fails to perform under this Agreement, 
the Seller may retain the earnest money deposit as liquidated damages, 
which shall be the Seller's sole and exclusive remedy.
7.2. Seller's Default. If the Seller fails to perform under this Agreement, 
the Buyer may: (a) seek specific performance compelling the sale, or 
(b) terminate this Agreement and receive a full refund of the earnest money, 
plus reimbursement of reasonable expenses incurred.

---

VIII. GENERAL PROVISIONS

8.1. Entire Agreement. This Agreement constitutes the entire understanding 
between the parties and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, 
and agreements relating to the Property.

8.2. Amendments. This Agreement may not be modified except by written 
instrument signed by both parties.

8.3. Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in 
accordance with the laws of the State of California.

8.4. Time is of the Essence. Time is of the essence with respect to all 
dates and deadlines specified in this Agreement.

8.5. Binding Effect. This Agreement shall be binding upon and inure to the 
benefit of the parties and their respective heirs, successors, and assigns.
