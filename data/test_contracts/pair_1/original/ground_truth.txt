MASTER SERVICE AGREEMENT

This Master Service Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into as of the 
1st day of January, 2024 (the "Effective Date"), by and between Alpha Corp 
(the "Client"), with its principal place of business in New York, and Beta 
Solutions LLC (the "Provider"), with its principal place of business in 
Delaware.

---

I. AGREEMENT DETAILS AND GOVERNANCE

1.1. Scope of Work. The Provider agrees to perform the services for the Client as 
detailed in the Statement of Work ("SOW") attached hereto and incorporated 
herein by reference. Any changes to the SOW must be made via a written change 
order executed by both Parties.

1.2. Independent Contractor. The Provider acts solely as an independent 
contractor. This Agreement does not create any partnership, joint venture, 
employment, or agency relationship between the Parties.

1.3. Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in 
accordance with the internal laws of the State of Delaware, without giving 
effect to any choice or conflict of law provision or rule.

---

II. TERM, TERMINATION, AND SUSPENSION

2.1. Term. The initial term of this Agreement shall commence on the Effective 
Date and shall continue for a period of **twenty-four (24) months**, unless 
terminated earlier pursuant to this Article II.
2.2. Termination for Cause. Either Party may terminate this Agreement 
immediately upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches any 
provision of this Agreement, and such breach remains uncured for a period of 
**fifteen (15) days** following receipt of written notice specifying the breach. 
A failure to pay fees when due constitutes a material breach.

2.3. Termination for Convenience. The Client may terminate this Agreement 
for convenience upon providing **sixty (60) days** prior written notice 
to the Provider. Upon such termination, the Client shall pay the Provider for 
all services rendered up to the effective termination date, plus any non-cancellable 
expenses incurred.

2.4. Suspension of Service. The Provider reserves the right to suspend the 
Services if the Client is more than fifteen (15) days overdue on any payment 
invoice. The Provider shall provide written notice of suspension, and the 
Services will resume promptly once all overdue payments are received.

---

III. COMPENSATION, BILLING, AND EXPENSES

3.1. Fees. The Client agrees to compensate the Provider at a fixed monthly 
rate of **$15,000 USD**, payable in advance on the first day of each calendar 
month. All undisputed fees must be paid within fifteen (15) days of the invoice 
date.

3.2. Late Payment. Any undisputed amounts not paid when due shall accrue interest 
at the rate of **one and a half percent (1.5%)** per month, or the maximum 
rate permitted by applicable law, whichever is less, from the due date until 
paid.
3.3. Expenses. The Provider shall be reimbursed for reasonable, pre-approved 
out-of-pocket expenses that are directly related to the provision of the Services, 
provided they are supported by appropriate receipts and detailed documentation. 
Expenses exceeding $500 require prior written authorization from the Client.

3.4. Taxes. The Client shall be responsible for all sales, use, and excise taxes 
imposed by any governmental authority on the Services provided hereunder, excluding 
only taxes based solely on the Provider's net income.

---

IV. CONFIDENTIALITY AND INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

4.1. Definition of Confidential Information. "Confidential Information" shall 
mean all non-public information, including, but not limited to, trade secrets, 
financial data, business plans, and source code, disclosed by one Party to the 
other, whether orally or in writing.

4.2. Non-Disclosure Obligation. Both Parties agree to use the Confidential 
Information of the other Party solely for the purposes contemplated by this 
Agreement and shall exercise the same degree of care to prevent its disclosure 
as the receiving party uses to protect its own similar information.
4.3. Ownership. All work product, including all Intellectual Property Rights 
therein, created by the Provider in the course of performing the Services shall 
be considered **"Work Made For Hire"** and shall exclusively belong to the 
Client upon payment in full. The Provider assigns to the Client all right, title, 
and interest in such Work Product.

---

V. INDEMNIFICATION AND WARRANTY

5.1. Mutual Indemnification. Each Party (the "Indemnifying Party") agrees to 
indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the other Party (the "Indemnified Party") 
from and against any and all losses, damages, liabilities, and expenses arising 
from any third-party claim alleging the Indemnifying Party's gross negligence 
or willful misconduct in connection with this Agreement.

5.2. Provider Warranty. The Provider warrants that the Services will be performed 
in a professional and workmanlike manner in accordance with generally accepted 
industry standards. This warranty shall expire thirty (30) days after completion 
of the respective Service.
VI. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

6.1. Exclusion of Damages. 
Except for claims related to breach of confidentiality 
or indemnification, neither party shall be liable to the other for any special, 
incidental, punitive, or consequential damages (including loss of profits or data), 
even if advised of the possibility of such damages.

6.2. Cap on Liability. The total aggregate liability of the Provider to the Client 
for any and all claims arising out of or relating to this Agreement shall be 
limited to the total fees paid by the Client to the Provider during the **three (3) 
months** immediately preceding the date the claim arose.

---

VII. GENERAL PROVISIONS

7.1. Force Majeure. Neither Party shall be held liable for any delay or failure 
in performance due to events beyond its reasonable control, including but not 
limited to acts of God, war, terrorism, or strikes.

7.2. Notices. All notices required or permitted under this Agreement shall be in 
writing and shall be deemed delivered when delivered by certified mail or reputable 
overnight courier.

7.3. Entire Agreement. This Agreement, including all attached SOWs, constitutes the 
entire agreement between the Parties and supersedes all prior agreements, oral or 
written.
